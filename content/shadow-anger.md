---
title: Befriending Anger
type: shadow-work
---
Anger is often a messenger pointing at a boundary that was crossed or a need that went unmet.

When anger arises, pause before acting on it. Place a hand where you feel it in the body and ask: what is this anger protecting, and what does it want me to know?

Many of us were taught to swallow anger, so it leaks out sideways. Listening to it directly, without being run by it, turns it into useful information. (Sample content for demonstration.)
